> about
Alex McKenzie is a multi-instrumentalist, producer, composer, and improviser based in London. With a collaboration centred practice, their primary output is as a member of the experimental folk band Shovel Dance Collective, the post-rock group caroline, the polystylistic performance project Gentle Stranger, and the new music ensemble Search Engine Quartet. Though musicially varied, their work is constistently uncompromising, drawing from 'non-idiomatic' free improvisation, traditional folk music, noise, sound art, and experimental songwriting. They have released a number of critically-acclaimed albums, and perform live regularly both within the UK and internationally. They have performed at notable experimental music festivals such as Big Ears (Knoxville, Tennessee), Rewire (The Hague), INTONAL (Malmö), BRDCST (Brussels) Supersonic (Birmingham), Ultima Contemporary Music Festival (Oslo), Huddersfield Contemporary Music Festival, and been a resi dent artist at Supernormal (Oxfordshire) and Motel Mozaïque (Rotterdam). Their work has been featured in publications such as Pitchfork, The New York Times, The Wire, The Quietus, The Guardian, and as part of NPR's Tiny Desk Concerts series.

They have an ongoing duo projects with Tom Hardwick-Allan, Grace Black, Aga Ujma as Rectal Reaper, and Adélaïde de Alfaro as Ant Mill. In collaboration with artists George Lynch, Hannah Tavener, Joe Davies, and Tom Hardwick-Allan they created mixed media interdisclinpary performance pieces 12april (CCA Glasgow, 2024) and Friend City (London Performance Studios, 2025). Alongside these ongoing collaborations they have performed in improvised music ensembles with artists including Moor Mother, Elaine Mitchener, Able Noise, Black Midi, Mosquito Farm, and Eli Winter, and collaborated with the artists Jesse Darling, Agnes Scherer, Adam Christensen, and Plastique Fantastique. As a session musician they have performed and recorded with artists such as Metronomy, Martha Skye Murphy, Naima Bock, Kiran Leonard, Juan Wauters, Jennifer Walton, and Goat Girl. Alongside Tom Hardwick-Allan they have composed music for commerical use. They regularly curate experimental performance and music events across London venues and, since January 2025, are a member of The Horse Hospital Collective, a small team programming and running the London arts venue.

Alex is a workshop facilitor and experienced music educator. They have taught extensively within mainstream and special education, and as an instrumental tutor. They have facilitated workshops at Slade School of Fine Art, Goldsmiths, University of London, and The Horse Hospital. They achieved a distinction in MMus Sonic Arts at Goldsmiths, University of London in 2019, and graduated with a 1st class BA (Hons) in Philosophy from King's College London in 2016.
